Wayfind

Hook

My capstone project will continue and build upon a ‘future interactions’ app (conceived in my Experimental Interaction course) which employs augmented reality technologies in service of safer and more enjoyable wayfinding experiences on the hiking trail.

Problem Statement

Over 51 million hikers, backpackers, and mountain bikers enjoy use of America’s 232,000+ miles of trail systems annually. Trail usage not only brings us joy, but has proven correlation to increased physical activity, mental wellness, and community health. However, a gap exists between the amount of existing trails, and funding provided for the maintenance of these trails – creating a trail maintenance backlog. Wayfind makes up for that gap by minimizing hiker uncertainty and creating an outdoor experience users can rely on.

Trails not maintained to quality standards can result in inhibited trail use. While this project does not purport to help solve the root of this problem (funding, legislative prioritization), it does serve as a stopgap safety measure. Hikers can rest easy knowing that if a trail is washed out, snowed over, uncleared, not clearly marked, or otherwise poorly maintained or has low visualization, they will be taken care of.

It’s important to note the inherent tension of using technology on the trail. By their very nature, trail activities are proposed as solutions to our hectic, screen-centric lives. Bringing more technology to the trail could seem like the antithesis of what a user should seek out. However, Wayfind seeks to ease this tension by providing a minimally invasive service that allows hikers to focus more (not less) on the trail and the beauty around them. All of the benefits of analog are present (lo-fi, simple, minimal thought needed, no ‘add ons’) while providing important safety features of the digital age to ease the mind and allow for a peaceful, immersive experience.
